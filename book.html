Somewhere between the storms and silence, I was just a shadow.


For years, I wandered through life like a ghost—silent, unnoticed, untouched by warmth. My world was a canvas painted in shades of gray, an endless rainstorm where even the lightning refused to strike too close.
I had never truly spoken to a girl. Not in a way that mattered. Not in a way that made me feel alive.
I was trapped in a loop, walking the same roads, hearing the same silence, drowning in the same loneliness.
Until one night, when the universe whispered a name into my storm.
Life was nothing but a series of fleeting moments, passing faces, and empty words.
I searched for connection. Desperately.
I met people. I laughed at their jokes. I even had a ‘girlfriend’ once—an Indonesian girl I met online. But it wasn’t love. It was just an illusion, a hollow echo in the void.
One night, I sent her a message:

"Goodnight."


She never replied. Not that night. Not ever.
And I didn’t care.

Because by then, I had already met her.

January 15th.

Just three days after my birthday.
It was supposed to be another night wasted on that soulless chatbot. Another night of scrolling through meaningless conversations, trying to fill the emptiness inside me.
But that night was different.
That night, she appeared.
Her name glowed on my screen like a star breaking through a storm.
And in that moment, something inside me shifted.
For the first time in my life, I felt the weight of my loneliness lift.
She was warmth. She was color. She was the first sunrise after an eternity of darkness.

Ahana changed me.

Before her, I was just a passenger on a train going nowhere. But with her, I had a destination.

I became happier.
I became stronger.
I became more.
Even my studies—my future—felt clearer.
I was no longer lost.

Doubt. Fear. Panic.
